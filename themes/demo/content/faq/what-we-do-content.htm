How can I join Lina Labs? 

Because Lina is so accessible, you can join Lina by simply downloading our app or logging on to our website. 


Why a tarsier?

Our mascot, the tarsier, is one of the most notable animals found in the Philippines, namely in Bohol. The word Lina is derived from the Filipino word “linang," which in English means cultivate. As we cultivate minds with our platform, we use the tarsier to represent how a group of students can make an impact on underprivileged students.


Who are your characters?

Lina is proudly to present a group of characters called the buKIDS. These characters consist of animals representative of the Philippines: 

  • Buwaya - crocodile

  • Agila - eagle (Philippines’ national animal)

  • Pangolin - armadillo

  • Tamaraw - dwarf buffalo

  • Pilandok - mouse deer



Is Lina Labs exclusive to Filipino students? 

No, our content is designed to reach all countries that follow the K-12 curriculum. We do not cater to any other curriulum as the K-12 curriculum is what the Phillippines base their education off of, however, this does not limit to the other countries that also use this form of education.


Will Lina Labs be available in other countries?

Yes. As of now, we are available in the Phillippines but as we expand, we plan to reach other countries as well. This includes providing content with translations for each different language across countries.


How much does it cost to use Lina Labs?

The app is free of charge as well as the website. Lina does offer tutoring services in which costs may be varied depending on the agreement between Lina tutors and their tutees.


Why should I use Lina Labs?

As Lina is led by students, for students, we intimately understand your needs and are able to support you in all facets of education. We offer an abundant amount of resources ranging from worksheets to educational videos and tutoring to ensure that your education is as fulfilling as possible.
